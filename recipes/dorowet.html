Doro Wat (Ethiopian Chicken Stew)

description

Doro Wat is a traditional Ethiopian chicken stew, known for its rich and spicy flavor. It is often served with injera, a sourdough flatbread, and is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine.

ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons berbere spice mix
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are golden brown.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and ginger, cooking for another 2 minutes.
  4. Add the berbere spice mix and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add the chicken pieces to the pot, stirring to coat them with the spice mixture.
  6. Pour in the water, cover, and simmer on low heat for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is tender.
  7. Season with salt to taste. If using, add hard-boiled eggs during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
