Genfo (Ethiopian Porridge)

description

Genfo is a traditional Ethiopian porridge made from barley or wheat flour, often served with a spicy sauce. It is a comforting dish, typically enjoyed for breakfast or as a light meal.

ingredients

  • 1 cup barley or wheat flour
  • 4 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • Berbere spice mix (for serving)
  • Honey or sugar (optional, for sweetness)

instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Gradually add the barley or wheat flour, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring frequently until the mixture thickens to a porridge-like consistency.
  4. Add salt to taste and continue cooking for another 5 minutes.
  5. In a small pan, melt the niter kibbeh and mix in some berbere spice.
  6. Serve the genfo hot, drizzled with the spiced niter kibbeh and additional berbere on the side. Optionally, add honey or sugar for sweetness.
  7. Enjoy your genfo with a side of fresh vegetables or as is!
